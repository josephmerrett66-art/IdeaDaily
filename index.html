The Science of Creativity
Most people die with their best ideas still inside them.
Not because they weren't creative. Because they never trained.

Creativity isn't a gift. It's a muscle. And like any muscle, it atrophies without use — and grows stronger with daily reps.
The Research
Quantity beats quality. Every time.
The Pottery Class Study — Bayles & Orland, 1993
A ceramics teacher split his class in two. Half were graded on quantity — as many pots as possible. Half on quality — one perfect pot. At the end of semester, every single best pot came from the quantity group. They'd learned by doing.
Jerry Seinfeld's Chain Method
Seinfeld wrote a joke every single day and marked an X on a calendar. His only rule: don't break the chain. He didn't aim for a great joke. Just a joke. Quality emerged from consistency.
More Evidence
Edison ran idea quotas. So should you.
Thomas Edison
Edison held over 1,000 patents and set idea quotas for himself and his team. He believed creativity was a pure numbers game — the more ideas you generate, the better your odds of a great one.
James Webb Young — A Technique for Producing Ideas, 1940
Ideas are just new combinations of existing concepts. The more raw material you feed your brain daily, the better your combinations become. Volume is the engine of insight.
